# Short answer
L-1A executives or managers may apply for a green card under the EB-1C category without labor certification if they continue to serve in an executive or managerial capacity for a qualifying multinational organization.

# Detailed guidance
- EB-1C classification under INA §203(b)(1)(C).  
- Must have one year of qualifying foreign employment within the past three years.  
- The U.S. employer must have a qualifying relationship (parent, affiliate, or subsidiary).  
- No PERM required.  
- I-140 filed directly by U.S. employer, followed by I-485 or consular processing.  

# Key requirements
- Qualifying multinational relationship.  
- Executive or managerial capacity.  
- One year foreign employment.  
- I-140 filing under EB-1C.  

# Atomic claims
- L-1A managers or executives may apply for EB-1C green card without labor certification (critical: true)
- Employer must demonstrate qualifying multinational relationship (critical: true)
- EB-1C classification is limited to executives and managers (critical: false)
