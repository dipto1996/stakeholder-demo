# Short answer
An F-2 dependent can change to F-1 status by filing Form I-539 with a new SEVIS I-20 issued by a U.S. school and proof of financial support before the F-2 I-94 expires.

# Detailed guidance
- Obtain Form I-20 from SEVP-certified institution.  
- Pay SEVIS I-901 fee.  
- File Form I-539 to request change of status.  
- Maintain F-2 status until approval.  
- Alternatively, travel abroad and apply for F-1 visa at a U.S. consulate.  

# Key requirements
- Valid F-2 status.  
- SEVIS I-20 and SEVIS fee payment.  
- I-539 filing before I-94 expiry.  
- Financial and academic documentation.  

# Atomic claims
- F-2 dependents must obtain SEVIS I-20 to change to F-1 status (critical: true)
- Status change can be done via Form I-539 or consular processing (critical: true)
- Applicants must maintain F-2 status until F-1 approval (critical: false)
